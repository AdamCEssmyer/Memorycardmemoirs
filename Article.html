Barrel Rolls and Bombs

Author: Adam Essmyer, Published:April 25th, 2023

Game: Starfox 64
Publisher: Nintendo
Developer: Nintendo EAD
Release Date: June 30, 1997(North America)
A fox. A falcon. A frog. A rabbit. Spaceships. Barrel rolls. Bombs. If you're familiar with these terms, you know where I'm headed. Starfox is one of Nintendo's most beloved franchises, though in recent years has been neglected, for reasons that we can speculate on later. But in this first episode of Memory Card Memoirs, we're going to take a look at my personal favorite in the series, Starfox 64. We'll be examining it's history and development, it's gameplay and story, and my nostalgia and own history with the game. And just as a side note- If you happened to stumble upon this video, thank you for giving it a chance, and your willingness to sit and listen to another 30 something year old man-child talk about video games on the internet. Now let's roll into Starfox 64. (See what I did there.)

Starfox 64 released on the Nintendo 64 in North America on June 30th, 1997. Directed by Takao Shimizu, produced and designed by the legendary Shigeru Miyamoto, written by Mistuhiro Takano, and scored by the greatest video game composer, Koji Kondo. The game entered development during the time development for Starfox 2 on the Super Nintendo was just wrapping up( which was ultimately canceled due to the N64's impending release in order to compete with the technical and graphically superior Sega Saturn and Sonly Playstation consoles).
